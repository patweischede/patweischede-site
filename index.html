Background

I didn't start in tech.
I started in immigration.

My grandparents migrated to New Zealand in the 1990s. I moved with my mum in the early 2000s. Temporary visas, years of uncertainty, the long road to residence. I lived the immigration process before I built a career advising on it.

Today I'm a fully licensed immigration adviser (IAA #202300030) operating under NZ Immigration Help Service Ltd, a family practice with over 20 years of operation and 18,000+ clients assisted.

But running a professional services practice taught me something else entirely: the operational side of advisory work is full of bottlenecks that technology can eliminate. Manual lead triage. Repetitive client comms. Invoice admin. Content production that takes days when it should take minutes.

So I started building. AI lead scoring. Automated email flows. Code-built websites. Invoice pipelines. Content systems. Everything I deploy runs inside a real practice first. If it works there, it works anywhere.

How I Work

From bottleneck
to infrastructure.

You tell me what's manual, what breaks, and what doesn't scale. I build the system that fixes it.

01 — Audit
Map the current state
Walk through your workflow end to end. Where are leads coming from? What happens after first contact? Where do things get stuck, dropped, or duplicated? What's taking your time that shouldn't be?
02 — Architect
Design the system
Choose the tools, map the data flow, define the triggers and routing. You get a clear blueprint before any building starts. No scope creep, no guesswork.
03 — Build
Deploy and test
Build the automations, connect the integrations, deploy the infrastructure. Test with real data before anything goes live. If it doesn't work reliably, it doesn't ship.
04 — Iterate
Monitor and improve
Nothing is set-and-forget. Track what's working, identify failure points, optimise continuously. Systems get better over time, not just at launch.
